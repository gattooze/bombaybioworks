Our Thesis

India's industrial sector burns fossil fuels because no credible, scalable alternative has been built. We are changing that — converting bamboo and agricultural biomass into high-specification solid fuels, biochar, and activated carbon products that meet industrial and export-grade standards, while simultaneously building the feedstock supply chain that makes it all viable at scale.

What We Are Building

Two Verticals.

01
Primary Vertical
Future Fuels

Biomass — bamboo stems, harvest residue, and agri-waste — is one of the most abundant and underutilised industrial inputs in India. We convert it into a range of high-specification fuel and carbon products that replace fossil inputs across industrial boilers, steel production, water treatment, and soil application.

Biomass Pellets & Briquettes — high-specification solid fuels for industrial boilers, co-firing, and process heat — domestic consumption and export markets
Biochar & Black Pellets — torrefied, high energy-density products for steel PCI markets and premium export to Japan, South Korea, and Europe
Activated Carbon — bamboo-derived activated carbon for water filtration, specialty chemicals, and industrial purification
Bio-fertiliser — returning nutrients to the soil and closing the agricultural cycle, making feedstock cultivation regenerative by design
02
Enabling Vertical
Ecosystem Development

The economics of biomass fuels depend entirely on a reliable, quality-consistent, traceable feedstock supply. We are building that supply chain from the ground up — establishing bamboo as a serious commercial crop with the cultivation infrastructure, farmer systems, and quality controls that industrial buyers require.

Commercial Plantation Development — end-to-end support for bamboo plantation establishment across suitable geographies to secure feedstock at scale
Sustainable Harvesting Systems — species-specific protocols, age grading, and recognition tools ensuring optimal 3-year harvest cycles and fuel-grade quality
GI-Mapped & Traceable Supply — geographic indication mapping for full traceability — critical for export market certification and premium pricing
Farmer Empowerment — tools, equipment, and knowledge systems for quality grading and supply chain participation at the village level

The Bigger Picture

Built for India's
ESG Economy

Both of our verticals are sustainability plays — not as a positioning strategy, but as their fundamental thesis. Together, they address two of the most material dimensions of India's resource and energy transition.

Sustainable Energy
Future Fuels as a Fossil Replacement

India's industrial sector remains heavily dependent on fossil fuels for process heat and co-firing. Bamboo and agri-residue biomass — converted into pellets, biochar, and black pellets — provides a verified, carbon-neutral alternative at industrial scale. These are the fuels that help India's manufacturers decarbonise without disrupting production, serving both domestic demand and export markets in Japan, South Korea, and Europe.

Sustainable Materials
Bamboo as a Resource Alternative

Bamboo sequesters carbon faster than almost any other plant, matures in 3–5 years versus 30+ for timber, and can substitute wood, plastic, and resource-intensive materials across construction, textiles, and packaging. Building India's bamboo economy reduces pressure on forests, supports rural livelihoods, and positions India as a credible global supplier of traceable, certified sustainable raw materials.

India's NDC commitments, SEBI's ESG disclosure mandates, and the growing appetite of global buyers for traceable, certified sustainable supply chains are creating structural demand for exactly what we are building.
